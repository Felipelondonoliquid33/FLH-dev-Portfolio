▶ About the Project


Cine Casero is an Uruguayan audiovisual collective dedicated to preserving family films and community memory. The website design translates an analog, cinematic identity into a digital experience — merging editorial typography, film grain aesthetics, and GSAP-powered scroll interactions into a cohesive digital space.

The core design challenge was building a digital experience that feels as warm and textured as analog film. Every scroll interaction was choreographed with GSAP SplitText to reveal characters as if being projected frame by frame.

The gallery section employs a multi-column parallax grid where images scroll at different speeds, creating depth. The testimonial cards use magnetic hover effects and grayscale-to-color transitions. Film grain noise is rendered as an animated SVG layer fixed to the viewport.

A custom marquee component runs in opposing directions using pure CSS transforms. The footer uses viewport-relative font sizes for contact links — growing to 9vh — ensuring they feel monumental at any screen size.

▶ Design Direction


Led the UX/UI design and creative direction for cinecasero.uy, translating a community-rooted mission — preserving analog family film archives — into a premium editorial web experience. The challenge was designing something that feels warm and handcrafted, yet technically sophisticated.


The gallery section is the visual centerpiece: images scroll at different parallax rates in a 5-column grid, with a central video overlay that expands on hover. The dark testimonials section uses magnetic card effects coded in vanilla JavaScript — cards subtly follow the cursor, creating an immersive interaction layer.


The "El Gran Film del Uruguay" section features a full-screen rotating film reel (GSAP rotation loop), with the title text rendered at 120px in Ivy Mode serif — a deliberate scale choice that demands attention and frames the historical weight of the project.

Film grain animated noise overlay, film tape strip decorators, mix-blend-mode luminosity text on video, clip-path gallery reveals, and CSS custom cursor behavior.

Full-screen overlay menu with animated navigation links, social links, dark background with ambient imagery, and backdrop blur opacity effects.
