▶ Background


Puente de Equidad features "La Carrera Invisible," an educational, neo-brutalist side-scrolling game simulating the systemic inequalities, double-shift burdens of unpaid domestic work, and economic barriers faced by single mothers in Bogota, Colombia. The project highlights time-poverty and structural issues through interactive checkpoints where players must answer quiz questions about domestic care and gender parity to progress.

▶ Key Mechanics


Time Poverty Simulation: Players must balance energy, time, and care duties, illustrating the real-world strain of care labor. Obstacle Course: economic gaps, childcare costs, and glass ceiling barriers that Esperanza must jump over to reach work.


Quiz Checkpoints: Educational questions on gender indicators. Correct answers clear the path, while incorrect answers double the difficulty of economic and time obstacles.
